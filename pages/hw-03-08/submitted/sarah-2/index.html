"
I have two bits of advice for my younger self.
"
The misconception is that without deep content, design is reduced to pure style, a bag of dubious tricks.
In graphic design circles,

form-follows-function

is reconfigured as

form-follows-content.

If content is the source of form, always preceding it and imbuing it with meaning,

First, I didn’t understand until later in my career how important it is as a designer to listen. That is the first thing I’d tell myself.

form without content (as if that were even possible) is some kind of

empty shell.
Listening is so important, and I feel like a lot of new designers don’t understand that. It takes time.
This false dichotomy has circulated for so long that we have started to believe it ourselves.
It’s important to have realistic expectations
to accept the fact that developing content is more essential than shaping it.
A Little Bit of Sound Advice is a work that reconstructs two separate texts: the first, a commentary on design and content authored by Michael Rock, and the second, an excerpt from an interview with graphic designer Rob Giampietro. Rock’s piece, titled Fuck Content, asserts that the validity of design should not wholly depend on the quality of its content. To further explore this idea, these texts were cut and recombined to purposefully clash with each other. Giampietro’s writing, which emerges from a traditional interview format, is illustrated in a serif typeface, whereas Rock’s radical piece is set in sans serif. The reading experience is stilted, with each piece scrambling for attention. The different texts take turns dominating the narrative, which is shown through hidden texts (visible through hover), color, and line-stricken sentences. In the end, two excerpts from both pieces are taken wildly out of context and merged into one sentence, constructing a new narrative of its own. Sarah Im 2016.
