DIVEST?

To remove all investment assets
from companies involved in
extracting fossil fuels in an attempt
to make progress towards climate
change solutions.

BACKGROUND

Despite having been proven to pollute the
air, land, and water, fossil fuels will still account for 78% of global energy use in 2040. Prolonged reliance on this source of energy will continue to negatively impact the environment through
the future.

Now is the time to divest and
instead invest in renewable
energy because the future
depends on our efforts in reducing
the carbon footprint in order to
combat climate change.

GOAL

The New School (TNS) and Rhode Island
School of Design (RISD) have come together to
create an informative flyer and invitation
about our initiative.

We hope to encourage a number of other
U.S. universities to combat climate change by divesting from fossil fuels and investing
money elsewhere in renewable energy sources. Receivers will be learning the benefits of divesting, hoping they will join our effort.

BRAINSTORMING

Collaborated with Sadie and Sarah from
TNS’s “Design for Journalists” class by writing
postcards to each other every week.

FORM

The flow of information will be intuitive in the design. The reader receives an envelope in the mail with an illustration on the outside. They then unfold the letter which open into a poster form. Finally they will receive a small invitation card inside asking them to join our efforts.

VISUAL INSPIRATION

Inspired by handmade—like textures to reflect
traditional letters and the qualities + sensation
of the issue.

PROTOTYPING

“BREAKING NEWS” POSTER

SPREADING THE NEWS

This invitation will be sent from our campuses to other universities that we are inviting to join our initiative with the intention of inspiring them to explore the benefits of divesting.

We have corresponded as digital and print
penpals in order to collaborate on an editorial poster project that conveys a news story based
in the future to an audience we developed.
