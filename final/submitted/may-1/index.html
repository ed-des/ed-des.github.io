Reading the City Through the Decades

Five articles,
Five decades,
Five maps of New York City.

Travel back in time and experience the New York City of each decade since the first published issue of New York Magazine. “Reading the City Through the Decades” is an experiential archive of five articles that show the historical culture of the city. Discover your city’s past and remember the struggles and triumphs of the people that made it the metropolis of today.

Two devices,
Two different experiences.

To fully experience the time travel, visit the website on both your desktop and mobile devices. The two web experiences are exclusively designed for its respective platforms.

Desktop Web Experience

The desktop web experience is purely literary. Pick a decade and read an article from that time period, side by side with the cultural and historical context of what the city and country was going through.

LANDING PAGE. The website is designed as a split-screen experience. Each half of the screen has its own scroll.
The left side introduces the user to the website.
The right side informs the user of the unique mobile experience they could have on the same website.
The list of the years are links to the articles from that time. Hover over the years to see the article title on the right.
THE ARTICLE. On the left is the article from the year. On the right is historical context of the city during that decade.
Separate scrolls.
THE MENU. When the menu icon on the top right is clicked, the menu appears on the left.
View the article title on hover.

How the double scroll works

the ARTICLE
The money has gone back to the people who own it, leaving the rest of us scared, mad, and faced with a basic problem. A real problem. Last year’s question — How is society going to survive? — seems a luxurious intellectual diversion. The question now is: How am I going to make enough, save enough, pinch back enough to survive the summer?
Surly summer. People who thought they were getting somewhere, achieving their private happiness goal of $15,000 or even $25,000 a year, all of a sudden find they can’t afford to go to the movies. Signs of stress are all over the place.
Unemployment is up 43 per cent from last year. One hundred resumes a day are pouring into executive placement firms; last year it was 20 or 30 a day.
historical context
By 1970, the city gained notoriety for high rates of crime and other social disorders. A popular song by Cashman & West in the autumn of 1972, “American City Suite”, chronicled, in allegorical fashion, the decline in the city’s quality of life. The city’s subway system was regarded as unsafe due to crime and suffered frequent mechanical breakdowns. Prostitutes and pimps frequented Times Square, while Central Park became feared as the site of muggings and rapes. Homeless persons and drug dealers occupied boarded-up and abandoned buildings. The New York City Police Department was subject to investigation for widespread corruption, most famously in the 1971 testimony of whistle-blowing police officer Frank Serpico.

Mobile Web Experience

Visit the same website on your mobile device to experience the articles spacially. Based on your geo-location, you will be able to unlock parts of the articles and the history of where you are. What was happening at this park in 1970? What historic event took place at this library? Compare the photos and stories of the past with what you are experiencing today.

LANDING PAGE for mobile.
MENU appears on swipe up. The years are links to the maps for each article.
For each article, there is a different map of landmarks that are mentioned in the article. The GPS location shows where you are in New York indicated by the yellow triangle.
Once a landmark is tapped, you are given information of what the landmark is as well as directions to get there.
Swipe up to go back to the map.
Once you arrive within the vicinity of the landmark, you are able to unlock the information about the place and read an excerpt from the article.
You are also able to receive phone notifications when you arrive near a landmark from an article while walking around in the city.
THE ARTICLE. Read the excerpt from the article about the location and about what the landmark was like during that time.
Swipe down to go back to the map.
Tap the icon on the right to see all of the landmarks from that article.
Swipe down to go back to the landing page.
