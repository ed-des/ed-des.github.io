Expanding our notion of screen.

Up until now, we have employed the web as a playground for a broader inquiry into deeper editorial design concepts, including: interaction, identity, adaptivity, time, and narrative. Now, let’s look beyond the web. How can we design editorial experiences by expanding out notion of the screen? Here are few examples to draw on. What are their objectives? Where do they succeed? The final project.

You will design and edit your own unique editorial experience.

Task

Choose one of the New York magazine articles below. Reimagine it as a serialized story, then design a new way for the reader of tomorrow to access and read it. Serialize it in one of the following ways:

  • Parts Over Time. All published for the same screen format, but with parts launched at different times.
  • Parts Over Space. All made available at the same time, but with parts published in different places, i.e. on different “screens” suitable to those places.

Challenges

To complete this design assignment, you must address the following challenges.

  • Editorial. Think of the original article as raw material. Take editorial license (cut, add, and reorganize content) to serve the reader experience you wish to design, and present it as though fresh and relevant.
  • Audience. Know who your audience is, and why your article is valuable to them.
  • Access. Not only are you designing the article’s reader experience. You are designing the user experience, i.e. reimagining how a user discovers/accesses/browses articles.
  • Identity System. What editorial identity unifies the article parts of the whole across time/space?

Deliverables

  • Mockups of the Full Article.
  • Mockups of the UX. Demonstrate how it is accessed and used in context.
  • Writeup. Introduce your concept, and explain how your design tackled all of the challenges specified.
  • Web Presentation. A mobile- and desktop-optimized web article to formally present your work (i.e. the mockups and writeup).

Schedule

  • Ideate – due 4/12. Choose an article. As you read it, draft several themes and visuals that come to mind. Sketch 5 ideas for what your final project could be.
  • Propose – due 4/19. Propose your final project, roughly demonstrating how it would work and how it tackles the challenges.
  • Rough Draft – due 4/26. Present a first, rough draft of your mockups and write-up.
  • Clean Draft – due 5/3. Present a clean-but-rough-around-the-edges draft of your mockups and write-up.
  • Complete – due 5/10. Present an almost-almost-final draft of your mockups and write-up.
  • Submit Final Draft – due 5/17.
