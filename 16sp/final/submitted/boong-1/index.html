What they see on their screen…

Books used to be made out of paper. Made by anything from animal skin, to vellum, to plants along the roads. Paper was easily destroyed. People’s information and knowledge were lost from the world due to those unfortunate moments where paper was torn or burnt. Our history is not completed because it went into the air with those paper.

They had to turn the page with their hands. They turned the pages at great risk. It was very dangerous. Paper can slice flesh. They often got an injury called “the papercut.” This led to further infections, putting their lives even more at risk.

There were only two ways that the people in the past can record their place in the book. One was to use a rectangular 2" x 7" piece of thick paper called “the bookmark.” Bookmarks were dangerous pieces of paper, often times decorated. But they were very frequently misplaced, falling out of books and pockets.

The second method was to fold the corner of the book, creating a “dog ear.” It’s relationship to dogs is still unknown. There was a large group of the population who did not prefer this method.

In the early 2000s, when there was no light in the room, they could not see anything on the book. The only thing they could do was to feel it. It is still unclear what people did with them at those times.

The way we interact with book has become a weird history to those in 2080.

Just wonder about the next 60 years and what changes are to come. The experience of editorial page and a reading experience have evolved so much in time.

Just what is it going to be like… In another 300 years?
