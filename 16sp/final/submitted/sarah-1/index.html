You live in the concrete jungle known as New York. However, instead of enjoying the eclectic culture of the Big Apple, your main and only concern is surviving the summer through one of the worst recessions in the past couple decades.

This is the backdrop of the Summer Survival Kit.
Choose from four different characters (including one pair of twins) to play from.
Each character has a distinct background. Choose the avatar that you fit or relate to the best.
Your goal is to help your character survive through the hard times. Do this by keeping their liveliness, wellbeing, and social levels high and stable.

Boost wellbeing levels by reading Food & Living Articles.

Boost happiness levels by reading Recreational Articles.

Collect your articles and store them for future reading.

These articles are curated specifically for you.
Hailing from an eclectic variety of sources such as The New York Times and New York (whose titles are indicated at the landing page for each article), each piece is chosen both for its relevance and its interest level in regards to the recession.

By reading the articles, you are simultaneously boosting the overall health of your character and increasing your own personal knowledge of how to live well through difficult times.

But how do you boost your character's social level?
Social points are aggregated by making friends and sharing articles. Go to find friends on your navigation bar to see your possible connections. You can add friends through a linked facebook account, or simply by putting your screens together through the side-by-side option.
Meet new friends simply by being side-by-side.
Once you become friends, you can share your articles via the dialogue icon. Sharing articles allows you to collect articles faster and easier: and, in the process, helps you connect with your friends.
The more articles you collect, the more you increase your social level; the more you increase your social level, the more rewards you receive.
Ultimately, more rewards translates into an easier, happier life for your character during the taxing period of the recession. Sharing between friends isn't simply an exchange of this for that; it is a beneficial move for both parties.
This app exists on that basic premise: the belief that sharing is truly a gift that pays for itself.
This project presents the question of telling a given narrative ( Summer Survival Kit for Recession Casualties and Inflation Victims —New York magazine) through parts, distributed over either time or space. I chose to approach the text by distilling it to its fundamental message about growth through compassion. This app transforms the reading experience into a game, pulling the narrative out of its dated context and into one that is new, fun, and relatable. Users receive daily articles and thus gradually build upon their knowledge about surviving a recession over time. In this alternative reading experience, the user is able to improve her character’s wellbeing by sharing readings with others: allowing fellow app users to become closer through their interactions and essentially putting the original article’s moral into practice.
