04-26: Touching on User Testing

Do it. Early + often. Some > nothing.

I wish we could spend more time on it, but like I said, some > nothing.

You built a prototype last week. Whatever stage it is in, put it in front of a user and see how they engage with it. (We will break up in groups of 4).

Follow these protocol:

  • Let your user interact with your prototype for 2 minutes. Tell them to talk out loud about what they are noticing and what they are deciding to do at every moment. Do not say a word, even if they ask you questions. Just take notes.
  • Now ask: Was there a point when you felt like "Aha â€“ I get what this is about."
  • Then ask: Was there a point where you felt the most confused?
  • Then ask: How would you explain this site/experience to a friend.
  • Thank them for their time.

Now ask yourself:

  • Did your user use your prototype as expected?
  • What is the most important take-away from this experience?
  • Are there new questions you have about the functionality of your prototype?
  • Which questions were most effective

Now let's discuss how it went.
